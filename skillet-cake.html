🍌 Skillet Banana-Chocolate Cake

From “Mezcla” by Ixta Belfrage — one of the best cookbooks of 2022

This is a skillet cake, not a pancake. A one-bowl chocolate batter is spooned over caramelized bananas and baked. The result: soft, oozing chocolate center and sticky bananas on top. Serve hot with whipped cream!

🍽️ Yield:

Serves 6

🧾 Ingredients

Chocolate Cake:

  • 6 Tbsp (90g) unsalted butter
  • 5¼ oz (150g) milk chocolate, chopped
  • 3 oz (80g) dark chocolate, chopped
  • ½ cup (100g) light brown sugar
  • Scant ⅓ cup (40g) all-purpose flour
  • Scant ½ cup (50g) almond flour
  • 7 Tbsp (40g) cocoa powder
  • ¾ tsp baking powder
  • 4 eggs, whisked
  • ⅛ tsp fine salt

Caramelized Bananas:

  • 2 Tbsp oil (olive or sunflower)
  • 4 ripe bananas, sliced ⅜ inch thick
  • 2 Tbsp (30g) butter, chopped
  • 3 Tbsp maple syrup
  • ½ tsp vanilla bean paste
  • ½ tsp flaked salt

Vanilla Cream:

  • ⅓ cup (150g) heavy cream
  • ½ tsp vanilla bean paste
  • Pinch of salt

👩‍🍳 Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Melt butter and half of the chocolates over simmering water. Remove, add all remaining cake ingredients and mix.
  3. Heat oil in an ovenproof skillet. Arrange bananas and fry 3 minutes.
  4. Add butter, maple syrup, vanilla, and salt. Cook 5 more minutes to caramelize.
  5. Spoon chocolate batter over bananas. Bake 12 minutes until set on top and gooey inside. Cool for 5 minutes.
  6. Whip cream, vanilla, and a pinch of salt to soft peaks.
  7. Loosen cake edges, flip onto plate. Replace stuck banana slices. Top with cream and maple syrup. Serve warm.

💡 Cook’s Note:

If some banana slices stick when flipping—don’t stress. Just peel and place them on top! No ovenproof pan? Cook bananas first, transfer to a greased cake pan, pour batter over, and bake as usual.
